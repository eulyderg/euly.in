Tangent triple sum identity
A surprising trigonometric result I first heard about from this video by Dr. Barker!
If three angles form a euclidean triangle (sum to 180°=π), then they have this property where the sum of their tangents is the product of their tangents!

$${a+b+c=\pi}\implies\tan(a)+\tan(b)+\tan(c)=\tan(a)\tan(b)\tan(c)$$


Algebraic proof:
$$\small\begin{aligned}\tan(a)+\tan(b)+\tan(c)&=\tan(a)+\tan(b)+\tan(\pi-(a+b))\\ &=\tan(a)+\tan(b)+\tan(-(a+b))\\ &=\tan(a)+\tan(b)-\tan(a+b)\\ &=\tan(a)+\tan(b)-{\tan(a)+\tan(b)\over1-\tan(a)\tan(b)}\\ &={(\tan(a)+\tan(b))}{(1-\tan(a)\tan(b))\over1-\tan(a)\tan(b)}-{\tan(a)+\tan(b)\over1-\tan(a)\tan(b)}\\ &={(\tan(a)+\tan(b))(1-\tan(a)\tan(b))-(\tan(a)+\tan(b))\over1-\tan(a)\tan(b)}\\ &={\tan(a)+\tan(b)+(\tan(a)+\tan(b))(-\tan(a)\tan(b))-\tan(a)-\tan(b)\over1-\tan(a)\tan(b)}\\ &={-(\tan(a)\tan(b))(\tan(a)+\tan(b))\over1-\tan(a)\tan(b)}\\ &=\tan(a)\tan(b)\left(-{\tan(a)+\tan(b)\over1-\tan(a)\tan(b)}\right)\\ &=\tan(a)\tan(b)(-\tan(a+b))\\ &=\tan(a)\tan(b)\tan(-(a+b))\\ &=\tan(a)\tan(b)\tan(\pi-(a+b))\\ &=\tan(a)\tan(b)\tan(c) \end{aligned}$$
